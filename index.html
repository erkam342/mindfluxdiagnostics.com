Why remote EEG matters

Seizures and epileptiform activity are common in hospitalized and critically ill patients, and many events are clinically subtle or nonconvulsive. EEG monitoring helps teams detect seizures, characterize encephalopathy, and guide timely treatment decisions.

Supports higher-level inpatient care

Remote EEG interpretation expands access to subspecialty expertise, enabling hospitals to deliver higher-level neurological care even when local staffing is limited.

  • Improved access to epilepsy expertise
  • Consistent coverage across shifts
  • Standardized, high-quality reporting

Helps prevent unnecessary transfers

By supporting advanced EEG interpretation locally, hospitals can often avoid transfers driven primarily by limited EEG availability, keeping patients closer to home.

  • Reduced transfer delays and costs
  • Improved continuity for patients and families
  • Stronger ICU and NICU decision support

Clinically actionable results

Our reports emphasize clarity, clinical correlation, and relevance to management—supporting medication decisions and escalation when needed.

  • Seizure and status epilepticus detection
  • Encephalopathy characterization
  • Treatment guidance aligned with context
Bottom line: Remote EEG interpretation strengthens local hospital capability, improves access to subspecialty care, and can help prevent transfers that are driven primarily by EEG coverage limitations.
